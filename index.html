Profitability snapshot. Venezuelan banks posted strong profitability in early 2026, partly reflecting continued credit expansion and indexation of loan rates to the exchange rate. Banesco leads private banks with an annualized ROE of ~127% — extremely high by international standards, largely driven by Venezuela's inflationary environment which inflates nominal returns. BDV shows a more moderate ROE of ~65% given its very large equity base (CAR 84.48%). Microfinance banks show divergent patterns: R4 has very high ROA/ROE (small equity base, substantial loan income) while Bangente is more subdued. Data Sources & Verification

Every figure for all 20 banks was extracted directly from each bank's own Balance General de Publicación — the audited monthly/quarterly balance sheet that Venezuelan banks publish under SUDEBAN regulations. FX: Bs 511.9922/USD (FRED EXVZUS, May 2026 monthly average). ROE/ROA are computed as annualized figures from the Jan–May 2026 Estado de Resultados.

Methodology
Total Assets (Activo Total / TOTAL DEL ACTIVO)
Read directly from the balance sheet. Consolidated figures used where available.
Loan Portfolio gross (Cartera de Créditos bruta)
Créditos vigentes + reestructurados + vencidos + en litigio, before provisions.
NPL Ratio (Índice de Morosidad)
Créditos vencidos ÷ Cartera bruta. Stricter than some official morosidad ratios.
CAR (Índice de Solvencia Patrimonial / Índice de Adecuación de Patrimonio Contable)
As reported in Información Complementaria. SUDEBAN minimum: 9%.
ROE / ROA (annualized)
Resultado Neto (Jan–May 2026) ÷ Patrimonio or Activo Total, × 12/5 to annualize from 5 months.
FX Rate
FRED series EXVZUS: Bs 511.9922/USD, May 2026 monthly average. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System (US).

Glossary — Spanish Balance Sheet Terms → English

Maps the Spanish accounting terminology used in Venezuelan SUDEBAN balance sheets (Balance General de Publicación) to English equivalents and definitions.

Balance Sheet — Asset Side (ACTIVO)
DISPONIBILIDADES
Cash & Liquid Assets
Cash on hand, deposits at BCV (central bank), interbank deposits, and foreign correspondent balances. Equivalent to "Cash and due from banks".
INVERSIONES EN TÍTULOS VALORES
Investment Securities
Portfolio of government bonds and other securities, split into: para negociar (trading), disponibles para la venta (available-for-sale), mantenidas hasta vencimiento (held-to-maturity), and restringida (restricted).
CARTERA DE CRÉDITOS
Loan Portfolio
Total lending, split into: vigentes (performing/current), reestructurados (restructured), vencidos (past-due / non-performing), en litigio (in legal proceedings). Net of provisión (loan-loss reserve).
Créditos vigentes
Performing Loans
Loans with payments current — the largest and healthiest portion of the loan book.
Créditos vencidos
Past-Due / Non-Performing Loans (NPL)
Loans where payments are overdue. The numerator of the NPL ratio.
NPL % = vencidos ÷ cartera bruta × 100
Provisión para cartera de créditos
Loan-Loss Reserve (Allowance for Credit Losses)
Amount set aside against expected loan losses. Subtracted from gross loans to give net loan portfolio.
INTERESES Y COMISIONES POR COBRAR
Accrued Interest & Fees Receivable
Interest earned but not yet collected on loans and investments.
BIENES DE USO
Fixed Assets (Property, Plant & Equipment)
Physical assets: offices, branches, IT equipment, vehicles. Often revalued in hyperinflationary environments.
OTROS ACTIVOS
Other Assets
Residual assets including prepaid expenses, deferred taxes, and miscellaneous receivables. Often large in Venezuela due to FX-adjustment items.
TOTAL DEL ACTIVO
Total Assets
Sum of all assets on the balance sheet.
Balance Sheet — Liability & Equity Side (PASIVO Y PATRIMONIO)
CAPTACIONES DEL PÚBLICO
Customer Deposits (Public Deposits)
All funds deposited by customers: demand accounts, savings accounts, time deposits, and FX-related deposits. The main funding source for banks.
Depósitos a la vista / Cuentas corrientes
Demand Deposits / Checking Accounts
Instantly withdrawable deposits. Dominate Venezuelan bank funding (90%+) due to absence of meaningful term deposits under high inflation.
Cuentas corrientes según Sistema de Mercado Cambiario de Libre Convertibilidad
FX Free-Market Checking Accounts
USD-denominated accounts held at Venezuelan banks under the free FX market regime (Sistema de Mercado Cambiario de Libre Convertibilidad — SMLC). Key indicator of dollarization.
OTROS FINANCIAMIENTOS OBTENIDOS
Other Borrowings / Wholesale Funding
Interbank loans and other non-deposit funding from financial institutions, both domestic and foreign.
ACUMULACIONES Y OTROS PASIVOS
Accrued Liabilities & Other Payables
Accrued expenses, deferred income, and other liabilities not classified elsewhere.
TOTAL DEL PASIVO
Total Liabilities
Sum of all obligations owed by the bank to depositors and creditors.
GESTIÓN OPERATIVA
Operating Result (YTD Net Income)
The cumulative net profit for the period, shown on the liability side as a source of funds before being formally transferred to equity. Equals RESULTADO NETO from the income statement.
PATRIMONIO / TOTAL DE PATRIMONIO
Shareholders' Equity / Net Worth
Assets minus liabilities. Comprises paid-in capital (capital pagado), reserves (reservas), retained earnings (resultados acumulados), and equity adjustments (ajustes al patrimonio — large in Venezuela due to FX revaluation).
AJUSTES AL PATRIMONIO
Equity Adjustments (FX & Inflation Revaluation)
Revaluation gains on assets and FX items recognized in equity. Often the largest component of equity in Venezuelan banks, reflecting hyperinflationary accounting.
ÍNDICE DE SOLVENCIA PATRIMONIAL / ÍNDICE DE ADECUACIÓN DE PATRIMONIO CONTABLE
Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)
Regulatory capital as a % of risk-weighted assets (simplified). SUDEBAN minimum: 9%. Venezuelan banks show very high CARs (20–85%) largely due to large equity revaluation items.
CAR = (Patrimonio + Gestión Operativa) ÷ Activo Productivo × 100
Income Statement (ESTADO DE RESULTADOS)
INGRESOS FINANCIEROS
Financial / Interest Income
Revenue from lending (ingresos por cartera de créditos) and investments (ingresos por títulos valores). The primary revenue line for banks.
GASTOS FINANCIEROS
Financial / Interest Expense
Cost of funding — interest paid on deposits and borrowings. Very low in Venezuela relative to income due to near-zero deposit rates.
MARGEN FINANCIERO BRUTO
Gross Financial Margin (Net Interest Income before provisions)
Ingresos financieros minus gastos financieros. The core interest spread before credit loss provisions.
MARGEN FINANCIERO NETO
Net Financial Margin (Net Interest Income)
Gross margin minus loan-loss provisions (gastos por incobrabilidad). Closer to the US concept of Net Interest Income.
MARGEN DE INTERMEDIACIÓN FINANCIERA
Financial Intermediation Margin
Net financial margin plus other operating income and minus other operating expenses. Broader measure of core banking profitability.
GASTOS DE TRANSFORMACIÓN
Operating Expenses (Overhead)
Personnel costs (gastos de personal) plus general & administrative expenses. Equivalent to non-interest expense or "cost base".
MARGEN OPERATIVO BRUTO / NETO
Pre-tax Operating Profit
Intermediation margin minus operating expenses and other items. Gross = before other income/expense items; Net = after them.
RESULTADO NETO
Net Income (Bottom Line)
Final profit after all revenues, expenses, and taxes. Used to compute ROE and ROA.
ROE = Resultado Neto ÷ Patrimonio × (12/5) × 100
Gastos por incobrabilidad de créditos
Loan-Loss Provisions (Credit Loss Expense)
Expense charged to create or top up the provisión para cartera de créditos. Reduces reported profit but improves balance sheet quality.
Impuesto sobre la renta
Corporate Income Tax
Venezuelan corporate income tax charged on bank profits.
Key Ratios & Regulatory Concepts
Cartera de créditos / Captaciones (Intermediación)
Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LTD) / Intermediation Rate
How much of deposits are lent out. Venezuelan banks show very low LTD (~40–50%) due to high mandatory reserve requirements (encaje legal) at the BCV.
LTD = Cartera neta ÷ Captaciones × 100
SUDEBAN
Banking Supervisor (Superintendencia Nacional de Instituciones del Sector Bancario)
Venezuelan banking regulator. Sets minimum capital requirements (9% CAR), approves bank licensing, and receives monthly balance sheet filings.
BCV (Banco Central de Venezuela)
Central Bank of Venezuela
Sets monetary policy, manages official FX rate (tasa de cambio oficial), and holds mandatory reserves (encaje legal). Under US OFAC sanctions.
Encaje legal
Mandatory Reserve Requirement
Portion of deposits banks must hold at the BCV and cannot lend. Historically very high in Venezuela (40–85%), which explains low intermediation ratios.
FOGADE
Deposit Insurance Fund
Fondo de Garantía de Depósitos y Protección Bancaria. Government agency that insures customer deposits in case of bank failure.
Cartera inmovilizada / Morosidad
Non-Performing Loan Ratio (NPL)
Alternative official term for the NPL ratio used in some SUDEBAN publications. Equal to (vencidos + litigio) ÷ cartera bruta.
