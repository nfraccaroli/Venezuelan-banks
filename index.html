May 2026 snapshot — fully verified. These 11 banks hold a combined $13.55B in assets at the official BCV rate of Bs 511.99/USD (St. Louis Fed EXVZUS) — down sharply from Bs 52/USD in December 2024. BDV alone accounts for 46.6% of covered assets with an extraordinary capital ratio of 84.48% (Índice de Solvencia Patrimonial), reflecting large retained earnings. Among private banks, Banesco leads with $1.86B in assets; Mercantil shows the strongest CAR among large private banks at 31.77%. Banplus and Bancamiga show the highest NPL ratios (2.67% and 2.66%) — still moderate by international standards. Banco Exterior has the best asset quality at 0.27% NPL. All banks exceed the 9% SUDEBAN minimum capital requirement by a wide margin.

Data Sources & Verification

Every figure in this dashboard for the 11 banks listed below was extracted directly from that bank's own Balance General de Publicación al 31 de Mayo de 2026 — the audited monthly balance sheet that Venezuelan banks are legally required to publish under SUDEBAN regulations. These are primary source documents, not estimates or third-party compilations. The FX rate (Bs 511.9922/USD) is sourced from the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis (FRED series EXVZUS), May 2026 monthly average.

Methodology
Total Assets
TOTAL DEL ACTIVO, as reported. For banks reporting both Venezuela-only and consolidated (with foreign branches) figures, the consolidated figure is used for BDV; Venezuela-only operations used for others unless noted.
Loan Portfolio (gross)
Créditos vigentes + créditos reestructurados + créditos vencidos + créditos en litigio, before the loan-loss provision is subtracted.
NPL Ratio
Créditos vencidos ÷ gross loan portfolio. This is a stricter measure than some published "morosidad" ratios.
Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)
Índice de Solvencia Patrimonial / Índice de Adecuación de Patrimonio Contable, as reported directly in each balance sheet. SUDEBAN minimum: 9%.
Net Income YTD
RESULTADO NETO from the Estado de Resultados de Publicación, cumulative January 1 – May 31, 2026 (5 months).
FX Rate
Venezuelan Bolivares to U.S. Dollar Spot Exchange Rate, May 2026 monthly average: Bs 511.9922/USD. Source: Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System (US), FRED series EXVZUS.
Per-Bank Source Documents
What this dashboard does NOT cover. These 11 banks represent roughly 85%+ of total system assets but are not the complete universe of Venezuelan banks. Smaller institutions (Plaza, Sofitasa, Banco Fondo Común, 100% Banco, Banco Activo, Banco Bicentenario, BANDES, Banmujer) are not included because audited balance sheets for those banks have not yet been obtained — they are deliberately omitted rather than estimated. BBVA Provincial data supplied was a January 2025 filing rather than May 2026, so it is excluded from this version pending a current document. Additional audited balance sheets can be added with the same verification standard.
